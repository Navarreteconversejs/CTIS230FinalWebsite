Cardio

  • Why should I do cardio?

    It’s been found that trying to improve your cardiovascular health can lead to a reduction in your resting heart rate and blood pressure which means your heart won’t have to work as hard throughout the day. There have also been studies that indicate that cardio can improve the functionality of the brain, improve joint health, improve the health of your skin, increase your workload, reduces the chance of developing type 2 diabetes, can reduce feelings of stress, can improve energy throughout the day and improve your sleep at the end of the day by encouraging REM sleep. Let’s not forget that cardio also burns calories meaning that it can help you with your diet by aiding in your caloric deficit.

  • HIIT vs. LISS

    High Intensity Interval Training (HIIT) is a form of cardio that involves short periods of high intensity anaerobic exercise with less intense periods of recovery. Low Intensity Steady State (LISS) is a form of cardio that involves doing aerobic activity at a low/moderate intensity for a continuous period of time. It’s been found that each have good things about them as well as bad things. Some people prefer HIIT workouts because they take less time to complete than LISS workouts and some may also find them more interesting. Some may prefer LISS workouts because they are usually less physically demanding, and they enjoy the time they take to do this form of cardio. Studies have shown that LISS is basically equivalent to LISS in untrained individuals and that mild interval training is similar in difficulty to HIIT. HIIT at a very high intensity was usually considered less enjoyable than LISS but both HIIT and LISS resulted in decreases in enjoyment over the course of weeks. Try to keep it varied and interesting. You could implement both forms of cardio into your training.

  • What cardiovascular exercises should I do?

    The answer to that question is pretty simple, if you have something in particular that you’re working towards (marathons, triathlons, etc.) then you’d want to do more of what you’re training for. If you’re doing cardio for the health benefits or to aid your caloric deficit, then you have a lot of options. The main thing to keep in mind is that it should be enjoyable, and it shouldn’t result in injuries.
